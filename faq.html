Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Who can donate blood?

Most people in good health, aged 18 to 65 and weighing at least 50kg, can donate blood. Specific eligibility criteria can vary, so please check with your local blood bank.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days. Platelet donations can be made more frequently, up to 24 times a year.

Is it safe to donate blood?

Yes, donating blood is a safe process. A new sterile needle is used for each donor, ensuring no risk of infectious disease transmission.

What should I do before donating blood?

Ensure you are well hydrated, have eaten a healthy meal, and have a good night's sleep before donating. Avoid alcohol and fatty foods before your donation.

What happens to the donated blood?

Donated blood is tested, processed, and then used for patients in need of blood transfusions. This can include surgery patients, accident victims, and those undergoing cancer treatment.
